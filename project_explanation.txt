1. PROJECT OVERVIEW
========================================================================
Explain your project in 1 minute: This is an AI-driven personal wealth manager for the Indian stock market (Nifty 50). It predicts future stock returns using Machine Learning, detects market regimes (Bull/Bear) using Hidden Markov Models, and uses convex optimization to build a tailored portfolio based on the user's risk tolerance. It also explains its decisions via a Next.js dashboard using Explainable AI.

What problem are you solving? Retail investors often invest emotionally and lack access to institutional-grade, quantified risk management and portfolio optimization.

Why is this project important? It democratizes quant-finance, bringing algorithmic trading logic and ML-based safety checks to everyday investors.

Who are the target users? Retail investors, tech-savvy traders, and wealth managers.

What makes your system unique? The combination of Regime Detection (adjusting risk when the market crashes), Convex Optimization, and Explainable AI (SHAP) so the user understands the exact reasoning behind the AI's choices.

How is this different from Zerodha / smallcase / mutual funds? 
- Zerodha: Just an execution platform; it doesn't advise.
- Mutual Funds: Human-managed, opaque, high fees, slow to pivot.
- Smallcase: Static theme-based baskets. 
- My system: Dynamic, mathematically optimized, AI-predicted, and fully transparent.

What are the key components of your system? 
1. Feature Pipeline. 2. ML Predictors (RF/LightGBM). 3. Volatility/Covariance Estimators (GARCH). 4. Regime Detector (HMM). 5. Convex Optimizer. 6. FastAPI Backend. 7. Next.js Frontend.

Explain the end-to-end pipeline: Fetch historical Nifty 50 data -> Engineer features (RSI, standard deviation, volume) -> Train classifiers & regressors -> Export models -> FastAPI loads models & serves predictions -> Frontend requests portfolio weights & XAI details -> Display to user.

What happens when a user uploads a portfolio? The Backend compares their current holdings against the AI's mathematically "ideal" optimized weights, scoring their portfolio health and suggesting rebalancing steps.

What output does your system generate? Target stock weights (e.g., 15% TCS, 10% Reliance), market regime status (Bull/Bear), and SHAP explanations for the selections.


========================================================================
2. DATA & FEATURE ENGINEERING
========================================================================
What data sources did you use? Yahoo Finance (via `yfinance` library) for historical prices and volumes.

Why did you choose Nifty 50? High liquidity, lower risk of price manipulation, and highly representative of the Indian economy.

What features did you create? Technical indicators (moving averages, momentum), lagged returns, and volume anomalies.

What are technical indicators? Mathematical calculations based on price/volume used to forecast direction. Examples: RSI (Relative Strength Index), MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence), Bollinger Bands.

Did you use fundamental data? The core relies on technical/price-action data and derived statistics. Fundamental data can be added as an extension.

How did you handle missing data? Forward-filling (ffill) for non-trading days, and dropping initial NaN rows caused by rolling windows.

How did you normalize/scale features? Standard scaling (Z-score normalization) to ensure features with large scales (like Volume) don't dominate small scales (like RSI).

What is feature leakage? How did you avoid it? Leakage is when information from the future leaks into the training data. Avoided by strictly shifting target variables backwards and using chronological time-series splits (never random splits).

What is rolling window feature engineering? Looking back at the last 'N' days (e.g., 20 days) to calculate a metric (like standard deviation) for the current day.

How did you handle outliers? Winsorization (Target clipping)—capping values at the 1st and 99th percentiles so the model isn't skewed by extreme, one-off events.

How frequently is data updated? Daily at market close for batch processing.


========================================================================
3. MACHINE LEARNING
========================================================================
Which models did you use and why? Evaluated Random Forest, LightGBM, XGBoost, and Linear Regression. Tree ensembles (LightGBM/RF) were chosen because financial data is highly non-linear and noisy, and tree models naturally handle these interactions without heavy assumptions.

Why not deep learning? Tabular financial data has a very low signal-to-noise ratio. Deep learning (like LSTMs) often massively overfits this kind of data unless you have millions of rows (tick data). Tree models provide better baseline generalizations for daily/monthly data.

How did you evaluate model performance? Evaluated using Hit Rate (Directional Accuracy, e.g. did it predict up/down correctly) and Information Coefficient (IC) - the Spearman rank correlation between predictions and actual returns.

What metrics did you use? RMSE (Root Mean Squared Error) for magnitude, but in finance, Directional Accuracy and IC are much more important. 

How did you split data? Strict Time-Series Split (e.g., Train 2010-2020, Validate 2021, Test 2022). Unseen future data was kept completely separate.

What is overfitting? When the model memorizes the training data but fails on new data. Handled via cross-validation, limiting tree depth, and increasing minimum samples per leaf.

What is bias vs variance? High bias = underfitting (too simple). High variance = overfitting (too sensitive to noise). Ensemble methods find the sweet spot.

Why Random Forest? It aggregates hundreds of slightly different decision trees to reduce variance and prevent overfitting.
Feature importance in RF? Yes, it tells us which features (e.g., 14-day RSI) were most frequently used to split the trees, but SHAP is better for local explanation.

What exactly are you predicting? The 1-month forward excess return of the stock.
Why 1-month? Daily returns are mostly random noise (highly volatile). 1-month captures reliable momentum/reversion trends and avoids excessive brokerage trading fees.


========================================================================
4. VOLATILITY & RISK MODELING
========================================================================
What is volatility? The statistical dispersion/fluctuation of a stock's returns over time. High volatility = High risk.

What is GARCH? Generalized Autoregressive Conditional Heteroskedasticity. It’s a model that predicts future volatility based on past volatility.
Why GARCH over simple variance? Because financial markets exhibit "volatility clustering" (turbulent days are followed by turbulent days). GARCH captures this time-varying risk.

What is a covariance matrix? A matrix showing how every stock moves relative to every other stock.
Difference: covariance vs correlation? Both measure directional relationship, but correlation is standardized between -1 and 1. 


========================================================================
5. HIDDEN MARKOV MODEL (HMM)
========================================================================
What is an HMM? A statistical model where the system being modeled is moving through "hidden" states based on observable events.

Why "hidden"? We can observe the stock prices and volatility, but the actual "Market Regime" (is the economy structurally safe or crashing?) is a hidden underlying state.

Why use HMM for market regimes? It cleanly separates periods of high-return/low-risk (Bull) from low-return/high-risk (Bear) without us manually hardcoding rules.

How does regime affect portfolio? If HMM detects a Bear regime, the backend dynamically cuts the user's risk tolerance, forcing the optimizer into safer, defensive assets or higher cash positions.


========================================================================
6. PORTFOLIO OPTIMIZATION
========================================================================
What is portfolio optimization? The mathematical process of selecting the proportions of various assets to maximize returns for a given level of risk.

What is mean-variance optimization? Markowitz's Modern Portfolio Theory. You look at the mean (expected returns from ML) and the variance (risk from GARCH), and find the optimal blend.

What is Sharpe ratio? (Expected Return - Risk Free Rate) / Portfolio Standard Deviation. It measures return per unit of risk.

Why convex optimization? Because the portfolio variance math forms a convex bowl shape, algorithms can reliably and quickly find the absolute *global minimum* risk or maximum Sharpe mathematically (using solvers like SciPy).

What constraints did you apply? 
- Long-only (weights must be >= 0).
- Max 20% per stock (to prevent putting all money into one ML prediction).
- Sum of weights = 100%.

What is the efficient frontier? A curve showing the highest possible expected return for every level of risk. The optimizer puts our portfolio on this curve.


========================================================================
7. EXPLAINABLE AI (XAI)
========================================================================
What is explainable AI? Techniques that translate complex machine learning logic into human-understandable reasoning.

What is SHAP? Shapley Additive Explanations. Based on cooperative game theory, it calculates exactly how much each feature (like RSI or Volume) contributed to the final prediction.

Difference: SHAP vs feature importance? Feature Importance is global (e.g., "Volume is generally important to this model"). SHAP is local (e.g., "For HDFC today, Volume pushed the prediction up by +2%, while RSI pushed it down by -0.5%").


========================================================================
8. BACKEND (FASTAPI) & 9. FRONTEND (NEXT.JS)
========================================================================
Why FastAPI? Lightning-fast Python framework. Python is required to run the Scikit-Learn/LightGBM models, making FastAPI the logical choice for the API bridge.

How do you load model artifacts? The models are saved as `.joblib` or `.pkl` files during training. FastAPI loads them into memory on startup and calls `.predict()` when requested.

Why Next.js? Industry standard React framework that allows for rapid, component-based UI development with great performance.

How does frontend communicate with backend? Via RESTful HTTP API calls (`fetch` or `axios`).


========================================================================
10. PIPELINE & AIRFLOW & 11. SYSTEM DESIGN
========================================================================
What is a data pipeline? An automated set of scripts that Extracts raw data, Transforms it computationally, and Loads it for the API (ETL).

Why automate? Financial data changes daily. The models and matrices need to be rebuilt sequentially without manual human intervention.

Modular System Design: The models (ML logic), the API (communication), and the Frontend (UI) are decoupled. If the ML model changes, the frontend doesn't break.


========================================================================
12. EDGE CASES & LIMITATIONS
========================================================================
What if the market crashes suddenly? ML and HMM models inherently lag; they only react after the first shock hits the closing prices. Black swan events cannot be perfectly predicted.

What about transaction costs & slippage? The model assumes perfect execution. In reality, rebalancing perfectly to decimal percentages costs money via broker fees.

Survivorship Bias: If you only train on today's Nifty 50, you ignore companies that went bankrupt or were kicked out 5 years ago, making past performance look better than reality.


========================================================================
13 -> 18. PRACTICAL & RAPID FIRE CONCEPTS
========================================================================
Would you invest your own money? Yes, but only with a strict stop-loss overlay and transaction cost modeling prior to execution.

How to backtest? Walk-forward optimization. Train Year 1-5, Trade Year 6. Train Year 2-6, Trade Year 7.

Why is financial prediction hard? Efficient Market Hypothesis (EMH). EMH states prices instantly reflect all public info. ML aims to find the tiny, transient inefficiencies before others do.

Define Risk: The probability/magnitude of losing capital (Standard Deviation).
Define Return: The financial gain/loss on an investment.
Define Covariance: A measure of the directional relationship between two asset prices.
Define Diversification: Don't put all your eggs in one basket; mixing uncorrelated assets to lower overall portfolio variance.
Define Overfitting: When an AI memorizes the past perfectly but fails completely on the future.
